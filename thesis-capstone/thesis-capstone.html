MANDATORY ATTENDANCE POLICY: Failure to attend this orientation may result in the non-approval of your enrollment in Thesis 1 or Capstone 1 by the College of Computer Studies (CCS) Department for Summer 2026. Attendance will be strictly monitored.

Group Composition Reminder: In accordance with CHED Memorandum Order (CMO) No. 25, s. 2015 (Annex A), students should preferably work in teams of two (2) to four (4) members per group. Pre-arranged groups are encouraged before the orientation.

Capstone Project (BSIT)

Application-oriented project for BS Information Technology students with Specialization in Game Development.

  • Solving a specific problem for a client or industry using IT
  • Deliver a functional, high-quality software system or network solution
  • Must include a working, deployable system as final output
  • Group size: 2–4 members (per CMO No. 25, s. 2015)

Thesis (BSCS)

Research-oriented thesis for BS Computer Science students with Specialization in Data Science.

  • Discovery of new knowledge or a new algorithm/technique
  • To prove a scientific hypothesis or advance a computing theory
  • Must demonstrate original research contribution
  • Group size: 2–4 members (per CMO No. 25, s. 2015)
Orientation Agenda

What Will Be Discussed

Discussion of CCS Research Agenda

  • Focus on AI-driven, innovative, and industry-aligned research addressing real-world problems.
  • Develop ethical, inclusive, and impactful technology solutions (AI, games, data science, etc.).
  • Align projects with key sectors: health, business, technology, environment, education, and governance.
  • Produce practical outputs and future-ready skills through research, innovation, and collaboration.
